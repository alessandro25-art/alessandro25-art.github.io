Impact Assessment · SDGs · Supply Chain

ILLY, Social Impact Assessment

Measured the social impact of Illycaffè's education program in coffee-producing communities. Applied the Output→Outcome→Impact framework across three SDGs.

Illycaffè has been a Benefit Corporation since 2019. The project assessed one of their flagship programs: after-school tutoring, vocational training, and scholarships for children in farming communities in developing markets. We mapped outputs, outcomes, and long-term impact against SDG 4 (Quality Education), SDG 8 (Decent Work), and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities). The main finding was that the program reduces dropout rates and child labor in the short term, and improves lifetime income in the long term, but the data on the latter is still thin.

ESG Strategy · Benchmarking · People & Planet

EY × Rigoni di Asiago, ESG Strategy

Designed two sustainability initiatives for Rigoni di Asiago with EY: one on inclusive hiring, one on converting production waste into energy.

"Taste Beyond Limits" expands hiring of people with disabilities through NGO partnerships and workplace accessibility improvements. "From Waste to Energy" converts fruit processing residues into biogas, which feeds back into the production system as renewable energy and produces organic soil nutrients as a byproduct.

We benchmarked both initiatives against Barilla, Ben & Jerry's, Ferrero, and Carlsberg to understand where Rigoni could differentiate. Ferrero, for instance, has an Inclusion Index of 72/100 with a target of 75 by 2025, which gave us a concrete reference point for the disability hiring initiative.

Operations · Sustainability · Manufacturing

Ferri S.R.L, Sustainable Operations

Consulting project for an Italian SME making ironing boards and ladders. Looked at how they could cut costs and grow in Northern Europe by treating sustainability as a competitiveness lever.

Ferri S.R.L has 47 employees and about €23M in turnover. Family-owned, B2B, energy-intensive production. Under new leadership, the company wants to expand into Northern European markets where sustainability credentials matter more for procurement decisions.

We analyzed three main barriers: corrective-only maintenance (no preventive system), high energy consumption with no optimization in place, and supply chain dependencies on aluminum and plastic. For each, we proposed interventions ranked by payback period; their CFO's strict 2.5-year cutoff was a real constraint we had to work around.

Business Model · Circular Economy · Strategy

Exon, Circular Business Model Design

Group consulting project redesigning Exon’s business model from hardware sales to a circular, subscription-based printing ecosystem.

Exon makes printers and copiers for household and B2B customers. Facing declining demand and no end-of-life strategy, the company needed a structural rethink, not a marketing fix.

We proposed five business model patterns: Razor-and-Blade (recurring revenue via ink and software subscriptions), Platform-as-a-Service (centralised digital hub for B2B fleet management), Experience Selling (creative printing hubs for B2C users), Freemium (basic free access, premium tiers), and Mass Customisation (photobooks, calendars on demand). The hardest challenge was making the sharing-economy pitch credible for household users who default to owning a printer.
